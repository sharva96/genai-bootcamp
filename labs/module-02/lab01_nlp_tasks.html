🏷 Classification Tasks

Assign one or more labels to a piece of text. The "yes/no/which-category" family — among the most common and commercially deployed NLP tasks.

😊
Sentiment Analysis
Determine the emotional tone of text — positive, negative, neutral, or fine-grained emotion labels.
Input
"The product is decent but customer support was a nightmare."
⟶
Output
{"sentiment": "mixed", "score": -0.3, "aspects": {"product": "neutral", "support": "negative"}}
Business uses
Brand monitoringCustomer feedbackReview aggregationSocial media analysis
Modern approach: Use an LLM (GPT-5 mini, Claude Haiku) with a system prompt — no training needed. For massive volume: fine-tune BERT or DistilBERT for 100× cost reduction.
📁
Topic / Intent Classification
Assign a category from a predefined taxonomy. Used for routing, organization, and triage.
Input
"My password isn't working and I can't log in to my account."
⟶
Output
{"intent": "account_recovery", "subcategory": "password_reset", "urgency": "medium"}
Business uses
Support ticket routingEmail triageVoice assistant intentContent moderation
Modern approach: Zero-shot with LLMs handles 100+ categories without training. Provide the label list in the prompt. Add few-shot examples for tricky edge cases.
🚨
Spam / Toxicity / Safety
Binary or multi-label detection of unwanted, harmful, or unsafe content.
Input
"CONGRATS! You won $5000. Click here to claim NOW!!!"
⟶
Output
{"is_spam": true, "confidence": 0.97, "signals": ["urgency_pressure", "money_offer", "suspicious_link"]}
Business uses
Email spam filteringComment moderationPhishing detectionTrust & Safety
Modern approach: Use OpenAI Moderation API or Anthropic safety endpoints for general toxicity. For domain-specific spam (e.g. crypto scams), fine-tune a small classifier on labeled examples.

🔍 Information Extraction

Pull out structured pieces of information from unstructured text. Critical for data ingestion, knowledge graphs, and search.

👤
Named Entity Recognition (NER)
Identify and classify named things — people, organizations, locations, dates, money amounts.
Input
"Apple CEO Tim Cook met President Biden in Washington on March 15, 2025."
⟶
Output
[{"text":"Apple","type":"ORG"},{"text":"Tim Cook","type":"PER"},{"text":"Biden","type":"PER"},{"text":"Washington","type":"LOC"},{"text":"March 15, 2025","type":"DATE"}]
Business uses
Contract analysisNews monitoringCompliance screeningKnowledge graph
Modern approach: LLMs (GPT-5, Claude Sonnet) do NER zero-shot — just ask "extract all entities". For domain-specific entities (drug names, legal clauses), use structured output mode or spaCy with custom labels.
📋
Structured Data Extraction
Convert unstructured text into structured JSON, CSV, or database records.
Input
"Booking for 2 nights, June 15-17, deluxe room, 2 guests, request: late checkout, $450 total"
⟶
Output
{"check_in":"2026-06-15","check_out":"2026-06-17","room_type":"deluxe","guests":2,"total_usd":450,"requests":["late_checkout"]}
Business uses
Invoice processingForm digitizationResume parsingBooking automation
Modern approach: Use OpenAI Structured Outputs or Anthropic tool use with a strict JSON schema (Pydantic). Guarantees valid structured output every time.
🔗
Relation Extraction
Identify relationships between entities — who works for whom, what is a part of what, who reports to whom.
Input
"Sarah, the new VP of Engineering at Acme, reports to CEO John Smith."
⟶
Output
[{"subject":"Sarah","relation":"works_at","object":"Acme","role":"VP of Engineering"},{"subject":"Sarah","relation":"reports_to","object":"John Smith"}]
Business uses
Org charts from textDrug interactionsLegal due diligenceInvestigative research
Modern approach: LLMs handle this with structured output. For high-volume use cases, train a specialized model on triplet-extraction format.

✍️ Generation Tasks

Produce new text from existing text. Includes summarization, translation, completion, and creative writing.

📄
Summarization
Condense long text into a shorter version. Two flavors: extractive (pick key sentences) and abstractive (rewrite).
Input
"[20-page financial report with 8 key findings, growth metrics, risk factors, and forward guidance...]"
⟶
Output
"Revenue grew 32% YoY led by cloud (+58%). Two material risks: regulatory pressure in EU, supply chain disruption. Guidance raised to $4.5B for FY26."
Business uses
Executive briefingsResearch reviewMeeting notesNews digests
Modern approach: Long context models (Claude Sonnet 4.6 500K, Gemini 2.5 Pro 2M) summarize entire documents in one call. Specify output length and audience explicitly.
🌐
Machine Translation
Convert text from one language to another. Modern LLMs handle 100+ language pairs without specialized training.
Input
"We appreciate your patience and will resolve this by Friday."
⟶
Output (Spanish, formal)
"Agradecemos su paciencia y resolveremos este asunto antes del viernes."
Business uses
Customer support i18nContent localizationCross-border commerceReal-time translation
Modern approach: GPT-5 and Claude handle nuanced translation (formal vs casual, tone, regional dialect). For real-time / high-volume use Google Translate API or DeepL.
🔄
Text Transformation / Rewriting
Change style, tone, formality, or simplify complex content while preserving meaning.
Input (legalese)
"Notwithstanding the foregoing, party A shall indemnify and hold harmless party B from any and all claims arising from..."
⟶
Output (plain English)
"Even with the above rules, Party A is responsible for protecting Party B from any legal claims caused by..."
Business uses
Plain-language lawsMedical-to-patient textBrand voice rewritingAccessibility
Modern approach: System prompt defines the target style. Add explicit examples (few-shot) for brand-specific tone or unusual transformations.
📝
Open-ended Generation
Produce new content from a prompt — articles, emails, code, fiction, marketing copy.
Input
"Write a 50-word LinkedIn post announcing our $20M Series A funding. Tone: excited but humble."
⟶
Output
"Huge milestone today — we've closed our $20M Series A led by Accel. Grateful to every team member and customer who got us here. This fuels our mission to make X accessible to every Y. We're hiring across all roles. 🚀"
Business uses
Content marketingEmail draftsCode generationCreative writing
Modern approach: Specify audience, length, tone, and format. Use higher temperature (0.7-1.0) for creativity. For consistent brand voice, fine-tune on past content.

❓ Question Answering

Answer natural-language questions. Three main types: extractive (find span in a document), generative (free-form answer), and conversational (multi-turn dialogue).

📖
Extractive QA (Reading Comprehension)
Find the exact span of text in a document that answers a question.
Input
Doc: "Our refund policy allows returns within 30 days of purchase."
Q: "How long is the return window?"
⟶
Output
{"answer": "30 days", "confidence": 0.98, "span": [37, 44]}
Business uses
Help center searchDocument Q&ACompliance lookupLegal research
Modern approach: LLMs with RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) — retrieve relevant passages, then have the model answer from them. Always returns citations.
💭
Generative / Open-domain QA
Answer questions without a specific document — using world knowledge or reasoning.
Input
Q: "If I invest $10K monthly at 8% annual return, how much will I have after 25 years?"
⟶
Output
"Approximately $9.13 million. Using the future value of annuity formula: FV = PMT × [((1+r)^n - 1)/r]..."
Business uses
General-purpose chatbotsAI tutorsFinancial assistantsResearch
Modern approach: Use a reasoning model (o3, Claude Opus 4.7) for math/logic questions. Always cross-check factual claims — hallucination risk is real.
💬
Conversational QA
Multi-turn dialogue with context — the model remembers earlier turns and resolves references like "it" or "that".
Input (turn 2)
Turn 1: "What's our Q3 revenue?"
Turn 1 answer: "$42M"
Turn 2: "How does that compare to last year?"
⟶
Output
"Q3 2026 revenue ($42M) is up 28% from Q3 2025 ($32.8M). Strongest QoQ growth in 8 quarters."
Business uses
ChatGPT-style assistantsInternal data Q&ACustomer supportVoice assistants
Modern approach: Maintain conversation history in messages array. Use long-context models (200K+) for extended chats. For very long sessions, summarize older turns to manage tokens.

📚 Retrieval Tasks

Find the most relevant content from a large collection. The foundation of modern search and RAG systems.

🔎
Semantic Search
Find documents by meaning, not keyword. "How to cancel subscription" matches "ending my plan" — even with no shared words.
Input
Query: "how do I quit my membership"
Corpus: 10,000 help articles
⟶
Output
Top 3: 1) "Cancelling your subscription" (sim: 0.91), 2) "Account closure process" (0.84), 3) "Pausing your plan" (0.76)
Business uses
Modern search enginesHelp center searchRAG context retrievalRecommendation engines
Modern approach: Embed query and documents with same embedding model (text-embedding-3-large, voyage-3). Index in a vector DB (Pinecone, Weaviate, pgvector). Use cosine similarity to rank.
📊
Reranking
Take a candidate set from initial retrieval and reorder by relevance using a more powerful model. Two-stage pipeline.
Input
Query: "Python async best practices"
Top 20 vector search results
⟶
Output
Reordered top 5 with relevance scores, removing tangentially-related results that scored high in initial retrieval.
Business uses
RAG quality boostEnterprise searchCode searchE-commerce ranking
Modern approach: Use specialized rerankers (Cohere Rerank, Voyage Rerank) — they take query + document pairs and output a relevance score with much higher precision than embedding similarity alone.
🎯
Similarity Matching / Deduplication
Find documents similar to a given one — for clustering, recommendations, or removing duplicates.
Input
Doc: "AI startups raised $50B in 2025."
Corpus: 1M news articles
⟶
Output
Cluster of 47 articles covering the same story, sorted by similarity. Top match: "$50B in AI funding marks record year" (sim 0.94).
Business uses
News deduplicationPlagiarism detection"Related articles"Document clustering
Modern approach: Same embedding stack as semantic search. For clustering, use HDBSCAN or k-means on the embedding vectors directly.
