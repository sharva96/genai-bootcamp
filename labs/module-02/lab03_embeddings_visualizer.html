➕ Word Arithmetic — The Famous Analogy

Word2Vec's most striking discovery: directions in embedding space encode meaning. The vector from "man" to "woman" is the same as the vector from "king" to "queen" — capturing the concept of gender.

king
−
man
+
woman
≈
queen

More analogies that work the same way:

Paris − France + Italy ≈ Rome
capital-of-country relationship
walking − walked + swimming ≈ swam
verb tense relationship
Einstein − scientist + Picasso ≈ painter
person-to-profession relationship
hotter − hot + colder ≈ cold
comparative form relationship

Why this matters: The model didn't memorize these analogies — they emerged from the geometry of meaning learned during training. This is why embeddings power semantic search, recommendation systems, and ground every modern RAG application.

✓ Notice: The query "quit my subscription" has zero shared keywords with "Cancelling your monthly plan" or "Ending your membership" — but they correctly rank highest. Embedding-based search captures meaning, not words. Meanwhile a keyword search would rank "How to subscribe..." high because it shares "subscription" — but that article is the opposite of what the user wants.

2. Why does the equation "king − man + woman ≈ queen" work in embedding space?

4. Why are embeddings stored in vector databases (Pinecone, Weaviate, pgvector) rather than regular relational databases?
